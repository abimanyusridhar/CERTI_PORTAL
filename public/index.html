About This Certificate

The Cyber Security Threat Awareness Training (CST) certificate is issued by the to vessels that have successfully completed the mandatory maritime cybersecurity training programme, organized under the supervision of ISO Lead Auditors and certified Security Trainers.

Compliance is aligned with IMO Maritime Cyber Risk Management guidelines (MSC-FAL.1/Circ.3), the ISM Code (SOLAS Chapter IX), and ISPS Code requirements. Certificates are valid for one quarter following completion. Port State Control (PSC) officers, vetting inspectors, SIRE inspectors, and Flag State compliance managers can independently verify the authenticity and current validity of any CST certificate issued by by entering the certificate number below.

Validated Competencies
IMO & Flag State Compliance
Proficiency in IMO MSC-FAL.1/Circ.3 cyber risk management, ISM Code obligations, SOLAS Chapter XI-2, and ISPS Code requirements applicable to vessel operations.
Maritime Threat Intelligence
Ability to identify vessel-specific cyber threats including AIS/GNSS spoofing, ECDIS manipulation, OT-targeted ransomware, and phishing vectors targeting seafarers.
Shipboard Cyber Hygiene
Mastery of onboard cyber hygiene practices: secure USB media handling, network segmentation on IT/OT systems, password discipline, and social engineering defence.
Incident Response & Continuity
Knowledge of maritime cyber incident reporting procedures, CAMS notification protocols, and recovery plans to ensure vessel safety and operational continuity post-incident.
